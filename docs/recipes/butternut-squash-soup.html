Butternut Squash Soup

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 4 fresh sage leaves, slivered
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Â½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 (2-pound) butternut squash, peeled, seeded and cut into 1-inch chunks (about 1 pound prepared)
  • 1 (15-ounce) can cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups chicken broth

Directions

1. Soften: Heat oil and butter in a soup pot set over medium heat. Add onion, sage, bay leaf, salt and pepper. Cook, stirring now and then, until onion starts to color, about 15 minutes.

2. Simmer: Stir in squash and beans. Pour in broth. Bring to a boil, lower to a simmer and cook, partially covered, until squash turns tender, about 20 minutes.

3. Swirl: Scoop out 1 cup soup (squash, beans and all); set aside. Toss bay leaf. Use an immersion or standard blender to swirl soup smooth.

4. Serve: Return reserved 1 cup chunky soup to the smooth soup (providing a little texture). Reheat over low. Serve hot.
